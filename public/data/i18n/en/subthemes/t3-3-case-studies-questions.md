# Self-assessment

## Question 1

**What is the most effective long-term approach to reducing digital inequality in organizations?**

a) Providing employees with high-quality devices once and expecting them to self-learn.  
b) Outsourcing all training and digital transformation initiatives.  
c) Focusing only on younger generations since they adapt faster to new technologies.  
d) Embedding digital inclusion into culture, leadership, and continuous learning practices.  

✅ **Answer:** Embedding digital inclusion into culture, leadership, and continuous learning practices.  
Bridging digital inequality requires a holistic and sustained strategy. This includes leadership engagement, a culture of openness to learning, inclusive access to tools, and ongoing upskilling. Treating inclusion as part of the organization’s core values — not a temporary project — ensures lasting impact and equity.

## Question 2

**Which of the following best captures why digital inequality has become a central concern for modern organizations?**

a) Because unequal access to and use of digital tools affects productivity, inclusion, and career opportunities.  
b) Because technology is replacing all human roles in the workplace.  
c) Because digital tools are optional and only relevant for remote workers.  
d) Because digital inequality only impacts low-income countries.  

✅ **Answer:** Because unequal access to and use of digital tools affects productivity, inclusion, and career opportunities.  
Digital inequality matters because it directly influences who can participate fully in modern work environments. Unequal access, skills, and confidence with digital tools create barriers to collaboration, productivity, and advancement. Addressing these disparities ensures fairness, innovation, and long-term organizational resilience.
