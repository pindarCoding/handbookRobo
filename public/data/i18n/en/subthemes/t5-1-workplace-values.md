## Summary description of each generation’s values from research and external sources

| Generation | Core Work Values / Priorities |
|-------------|------------------------------|
| **Baby Boomers** (approx. born 1946–1964) | Dedication, stability in employment, appreciation for the chain of command, acknowledgment of expertise, responsibility, honour/status, and the intrinsic joy found in contributing to work. Prefer structured environments and traditional authority. External incentives and acknowledgment, such as titles and status, carry significant importance. |
| **Generation X** (born ~1965–1980) | Freedom, adaptability, and maintaining a balance between work and personal life. Comfortable with transformation and appreciate independence and self-sufficiency. Value fairness and sustainable work environments over job security. |
| **Millennials (Gen Y)** (born ~1981–1996) | Growth, purpose, inclusivity, and continuous learning. Appreciate diversity, open feedback, and collaborative environments with flatter hierarchies. Seek meaning and alignment with personal values. |
| **Generation Z** (born ~1997–2012) | Digital natives who prioritize technology integration, flexibility, purpose, and sustainability. Value transparency, continuous learning, and mental health. Seek meaningful, ethical work environments. |
